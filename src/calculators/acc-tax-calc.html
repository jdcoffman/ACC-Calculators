FAQs
What is the ACC tax rate?
ACC's adopted rate is $0.1034 per $100 of taxable value — about $103.40 per $100,000 of taxable value. Rates can change each year.
What does "taxable value" mean?
Taxable value is your home's appraised value after exemptions are subtracted. Your county appraisal district sets this number.
How does the Homestead exemption work here?
For ACC, the homestead exemption is the larger of $5,000 or 1% of your home's value. This is applied before calculating the ACC tax.
What is the Age 65+ / Disabled exemption?
ACC provides an additional $75,000 exemption for homeowners who qualify as age 65+ or disabled, on top of the homestead exemption. The $75,000 is applied once even if both categories apply.
If I'm 65+, will my ACC tax be frozen at a pre-ACC amount?
No. If ACC wasn't taxing your property before, there was no ACC amount to freeze. The tax ceiling would start at whatever you pay in the first year Pflugerville is in-district.
Does this affect renters?
Renters don't receive property tax exemptions directly. The main benefit for renters is in-district tuition access — lower rates for ACC courses, dual credit, and workforce training programs.
Do I need to reapply for exemptions?
Usually no — if your exemptions are already on file with the county, they should carry over. Verify with Travis or Williamson County Appraisal District to confirm.

Calculation: taxable value = max(0, home value − exemptions). ACC tax = taxable value × 0.001034. The additional $75,000 exemption is applied once even if both 65+ and Disabled are selected.
